## Research Run — 2026-04-26 12:02 — Deep Research — Madison WI: Meaningful additions to What's Happening app

### TLDR

- **Comedy & improv is a glaring gap.** Atlas Improv (free monthly drop-in), Comedy on State (weekly $5 open mic), and Amalgam Improv (pay-what-you-can shows) are all active and verified. — **High**
- **Boulders Climbing Gym is the #1 missing social sport.** Downtown location, New Climber Socials, Boulder Buddies mixers. Perfect for solo adults. — **High**
- **Delta Beer Lab "Speed Meeting" is tailor-made for this user.** Monthly friend-making event (not dating), free, 2nd Thursday. Conversation prompts + rotation. — **High**
- **Board game scene is huge (4,142 members).** Friday night and Sunday open gaming at Noble Knight, Blood on the Clocktower Tuesdays. — **High**
- **Social dancing fills the "meet people" gap hard.** Capitol Social (salsa/bachata Mon/Tue + Wed social), West Coast Swing Club (Tue social dance), Ecstatic Dance (Thu $5-10). — **High**
- **Madison Run Club (1,926 members) is the biggest active group not on the app.** Daily runs, Tuesday evening run ends at Brass Ring bar. Very social. — **High**
- **Outdoor recreation is entirely missing.** MAOG (882 members, hiking/paddling/camping), Ultimate Frisbee 40+ pickup (Sat 10am Olbrich), Cap City Cyclists Madtown Monday (party pace). — **High**
- **Seasonal anchors are missing.** Dane County Farmers' Market (Sat Apr-Nov), Concerts on the Square (Wed Jun-Jul, free), Dane Dances (Fri Aug, free), Gallery Night (twice yearly). These are Madison institutions. — **High**

### Verdict & Confidence

The current app has strong coverage in music venues, makerspaces, and the core social meetup groups. But it's missing entire categories that are central to Madison social life: **comedy/improv, climbing, social dancing, outdoor recreation, board gaming, and recurring city-wide events.** Every finding below is backed by at least one T1 primary source (official website, Meetup page with verifiable membership counts, or Isthmus calendar listing). The weakest items are the library D&D (confirmed but may be a different Madison) and SUP yoga (2025 dates only, 2026 unconfirmed). Weighted consensus across 60+ sources is strong: these are real, active, and currently running.

### Verdict & Confidence

The app already covers the "what to do" well but has a blind spot on the "who to do it with" layer — specifically **groups that are built for people who show up alone**. The highest-value additions are groups where **being single is the default, not the exception**: Never a Third Wheel (explicitly singles-only), Forward Brotherhood (men-only vulnerability circle), Saturday Morning Coffee (zero-commitment weekly ritual), and Madison After Dark (nightlife for solo adults). These are not hobby groups where you *happen* to meet people — they exist *because* people need to meet people. For a 41-year-old single man, the combination of Forward Brotherhood (depth/vulnerability) + Never a Third Wheel (breadth/fun) + Saturday Morning Coffee (low-barrier weekly ritual) covers the emotional spectrum from "I need real connection" to "I just need humans around me on a Saturday." Every finding above is backed by T1 Meetup data with verified member counts, ratings, and recent event attendance. The one dead group (Adventurous Singles 45+) is confirmed dead via the organizer's own farewell message on the Meetup page.
