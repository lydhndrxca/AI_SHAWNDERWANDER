# Golf Mind Game

A text-based golf game set at Pebble Beach Golf Links. The game isn't really about hitting the ball — it's about what's going on in your head while you hit it.

## How to Play

Open `index.html` in any browser. No server needed.

## The Game

You're playing the front nine at Pebble Beach with a guy named Dave. Each hole:

1. **The scene is set** — Real course descriptions of each hole, pulling from what makes Pebble Beach iconic: the cliffs, the ocean, the Monterey pines, the famous par 3s over water.

2. **You step up to the ball** — Click to swing.

3. **Your mind wanders** — During each backswing, you're presented with thoughts. Some are good golf thoughts (proper technique, course management). Some are distractions (the waitress, your boss, a sea otter). Some are anxiety spirals.

4. **Each thought has consequences** — Good golf thoughts tend to produce better shots, but bad/funny thoughts build personality traits that affect your relationship with Dave and the overall experience.

## The Trait System

Every choice adjusts your personality:
- **FOCUS** — Mental discipline
- **SWAGGER** — Confidence and style  
- **HUMOR** — How fun you are to golf with
- **GOLF IQ** — Technical understanding
- **ZEN** — Staying calm under pressure

These traits affect shot outcomes AND social dynamics. High humor + low focus = fun to be around but you can't break 90.

## The Social Layer

Dave is watching. Dave is reacting. Your choices affect his impression of you, which changes how he interacts between holes. Be too serious and he gets bored. Be too distracted and he gets annoyed. Find the balance and you might actually make a friend.

## Non-Linear

There's no single "right" path. Some choices are obviously better for golf, some are obviously funnier, and some are deliberately close — requiring real golf knowledge to distinguish.
