# Bumble — Strategy Playbook

> Tactical guidance synthesized from research + multi-platform context.

## The Core Insight

Bumble is the **opener-bait app**. Women send the first message; matches expire in 24 hours; everything in your profile is in service of giving her something to open with. Your bio + 3 prompts together should plant **3–5 distinct conversation hooks**.

For a 41M serious dater, Bumble is a **primary** channel — well-aligned audience, intentional matching, relationship-oriented women. Treat it accordingly: high effort, high curation.

## Tone Match

| App | Vibe | Tone to Match |
|-----|------|---------------|
| Tinder | Casual / fun / fast | Witty, confident, brief |
| **Bumble** | **Intentional / balanced** | **Warm, approachable, conversation-ready** |
| Hinge | Relationship-focused | Thoughtful, genuine, specific |
| FB Dating | Casual / community | Friendly, natural, relatable |

The biggest tonal divergence from Tinder: **Bumble does not reward edge.** Self-deprecation, sarcasm-as-personality, and Tinder-style snark all play worse here. Bumble's audience filters for "would I actually want to date this person, not just match with him." Lean warm. Polish without performing.

## Bio Architecture (300-char limit)

Bumble's 300-char limit is the tightest among substantive-bio apps. Every sentence must do work. The structure that consistently lands:

1. **Distinctive identity beat** — career arrow, unusual hobby, one specific lifestyle fact
2. **2–3 concrete creative/lifestyle nouns** — songs, pies, miniature TV sets, dog, etc.
3. **One self-aware humor or warmth line** — proves you're a real person, not a polished performance
4. **A soft closing hook** — implies what kind of partnership without explicit CTA

Avoid:
- Demographic stat-dumping ("41 from Madison")
- Adjective lists
- Direct CTAs ("message me if…", "let's grab coffee")
- "Looking for someone serious" (declarative; show via voice)

## Prompt Strategy

You get **3 prompts × ~120 chars each.** The ideal trio gives women three different opener surfaces:

| Surface | Best prompts for it |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Voice / humor signal** | "I quote too much from…", "My most useless talent is…", "The dorkiest thing about me…" |
| **Opinion / Compliment-bait** | "Give me your best hot take on…", "I'm convinced that…", "Change my mind about…" |
| **Relationship vision / soft filter** | "I'll fall for you if…", "The way to my heart is…", "Together we could…" |

**Don't pick three of the same surface.** Range = signal of range.

### The Compliments lever

In 2026, women can send Compliments (pre-match messages) tied to specific prompts. **Opinion-heavy and polarizing prompts attract more Compliments.** This means at least one of your three prompts should be opinion-driven — even if you'd otherwise default to all-warm.

## Algorithmic Levers (free wins, ranked)

1. **Verify (Photo Verification badge)** — +93% match rate. **The single biggest lever in any dating app.** Do it day 1.
2. **Fill every profile field** — completeness is the #1 algorithmic input. Use all 6 photos, all 3 prompts, all interest badges.
3. **Reply within hours, not days** — response velocity is a heavily weighted signal
4. **Daily activity** — 5–10 min/day beats marathon weekend sessions
5. **Use Opening Moves** — set a default question that gives matches an easy answer-prompt

## "Never Married, No Kids" Framing on Bumble

Per STRATEGY.md, the optimal Bumble framing:

> "No kids, never married — I was too busy [funny reason]. Now looking for someone worth slowing down for."

**Approach options:**
- **Bio:** Integrate as one line with a humor pivot ("…too busy [funny] / now looking for someone worth slowing down for")
- **Prompt:** Use "I'm looking for…" as the slot — "The actual one. Yes, I know — never been married. I am open to explaining."

Don't:
- State it as a defensive declaration
- Add "no baggage"
- Make it the bio headline

## What to Do, What Not to Do

| Do | Don't |
|------|-------|
| Verify on day 1 | Skip verification (you're throwing away +93%) |
| Fill all 3 prompt slots | Leave any slot empty |
| Plant 3+ conversation hooks across bio + prompts | Make it hard to message you |
| Use specific concrete nouns | Use abstract adjective lists |
| Use 1 opinion prompt to attract Compliments | Run all 3 prompts as humor |
| Reply within hours | Take days to respond |
| Use light humor | Use Tinder-style snark or sarcasm |
