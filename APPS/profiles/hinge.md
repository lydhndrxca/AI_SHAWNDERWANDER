## Writing Voice Strategy

Based on Research Run 3 (Writing Voice, Levity & Craft). These principles govern every prompt answer:

| Principle | What it means | Research basis |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Warmth > Cleverness** | Approachable beats impressive. Don't prove you're smart. | T1: affiliative humor outperforms aggressive for LTR |
| **Scene > List** | One vivid image beats three adjectives. | T1: narrative profiles → empathy → romantic interest (2026, 594 participants) |
| **Conversational Tone** | Contractions, first person, how you'd text a friend. | T3: authenticity markers → +31% meaningful replies |
| **Constraint Signal** | Admit one real, small imperfection. Shows confidence. | T1: self-deprecation safe when high-status is established |
| **Open Loop Ending** | End with something that makes responding effortless. | T2: likes with comments → 2x date conversion on Hinge |
| **Iceberg Depth** | Imply more than you state. Let them wonder. | Hemingway's theory of omission + microfiction craft |
| **Show, Never Label** | The writing itself demonstrates creativity. Never say "I'm creative." | T1: originality (metaphors + concrete details) → perceived intelligence + humor |

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Sarcasm/irony in text (loses tone, reads as negativity — T1/T2/T3 convergent)
- Adjective lists ("adventurous, funny, easygoing")
- Symmetrical sentence structure (AI tell — triggers uncanny valley)
- Abstract vague language without proper nouns or concrete details
- Proving you're funny (let the voice do it; save the comedy for voice prompts and dates)

## VERSION A — Just a Guy

Strategy: Sounds like a person, not a profile. Three real details from your life. No metaphors. No poetry. Just warm, slightly funny, and specific enough that nobody else could have written it.

### Prompt 1 — Humor (built-in game)

**Selected:** Two truths and a lie

```
I have a pilot's license, I own roughly 30 houseplants, and I've never burned a pie.
```

**Character count:** 84 / 150

*The lie (burned a pie) is relatable and funny — everyone's burned something. The truths (pilot + 30 plants) are unexpected range. All three are specific, concrete, and non-generic. Game format = people WILL guess.*

## VERSION B — Shows Up

Strategy: Reveals how he actually treats people. Micro-stories over adjectives. Admits real things without making a speech about it. The research says this version has the strongest alignment: constraint signaling, clear-coded values, and narrative over lists.

### Prompt 1 — Humor (micro-story)

**Selected:** The dorkiest thing about me is

```
I 3D-printed a friend a gift based on an inside joke. Took 40 hours. They said 'Oh cool' and put it on a shelf. No regrets.
```

**Character count:** 123 / 150

*Complete story in 123 characters. "Put it on a shelf" is a concrete, slightly sad image that makes it funnier. "No regrets" is warmer and more casual than "worth it" — it's how you'd actually say it.*

---

### Prompt 2 — Depth (clear-coded)

**Selected:** I want someone who

```
asks 'wait, why?' as often as I do. Bonus points if you have a strong take on something I haven't thought about yet.
```

**Character count:** 116 / 150

*Specific behavior ("asks 'wait, why?'") instead of adjectives like "curious" or "open-minded." "Bonus points" is playful. Ends with a direct invitation for her to bring something to the table.*

---

### Prompt 3 — Conversation Starter (shared picture)

**Selected:** Together, we could

find the best coffee shop in every city, argue about modern architecture, and attempt a recipe that's way above our skill level.

**Character count:** 128 / 150
