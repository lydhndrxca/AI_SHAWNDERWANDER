# Hinge — Research Findings

> Distilled from `APPS/RESEARCH.md`. Every Hinge-specific data point, with confidence levels and source tier.

## Headline Findings

### 1. Prompt likes convert 47% better than photo likes
- **Confidence:** Medium-High
- **Source:** T2 Hinge Labs internal research, widely cited (hinge.co/press/prompt-feedback)
- **Caveats:** Hinge's own data — potential self-serving bias. Methodology not publicly disclosed. Measures *conversion* (like → date), not initial attraction.
- **Implication:** Photos still gatekeep (initial attention). Prompts decide who actually meets you.

### 2. Voice prompts → 32% more dates and are massively under-used
- **Confidence:** High (under-utilization = competitive advantage)
- **Source:** T2 Hinge official newsroom + T2 Hinge dating data 2025
- **Implication:** Adoption is <5% of users. **This is the single biggest free lever on Hinge.**

### 3. Filling all 3 prompts = 73% more quality matches
- **Source:** T2 Hinge internal stats
- **Implication:** Don't leave any prompt slot empty. Ever.

### 4. Most Compatible feature = 8x date likelihood
- **Confidence:** High
- **Source:** T2 Hinge press
- **How it works:** Gale-Shapley mutual fit algorithm; daily curated picks. Treat these as priority likes.

### 5. Commented likes are the top algorithmic signal
- **Confidence:** Medium-High
- **Source:** T3 algorithm analysts (getmatches.ai), corroborated by Hinge community
- **Implication:** Photos and prompts that *invite a comment* outperform those that don't. Every prompt should end with something that's easy to react to.

## Two-Layer System (Photos vs. Prompts)

> Resolves the apparent contradiction "photos are 10x more important" vs. "prompts convert 47% better."

- **Layer 1 (gate) — Photos.** University of Amsterdam: improving photos increased matches 70%; improving bios increased matches 8%. Attractiveness has **7–20x** the impact of every other profile factor for the *initial* like decision.
- **Layer 2 (conversion) — Prompts.** Hinge's 47% stat measures the second step — once someone is attracted, prompts are what decides whether they actually meet you.

Both are true. Photos get the like; prompts get the date.

## Best-Performing Prompt Strategy

- **Combo:** 1 humor + 1 depth + 1 conversation-starter
- **High-converting prompts (SwipeStats):**
  - "Two Truths and a Lie"
  - "I Geek Out On"
  - "Together We Could"
  - "A Life Goal of Mine"
  - "I know the best spot in town for..."
  - "My simple pleasure..."
  - "The dorkiest thing about me..."
  - "Dating me is like..."
- **Mechanics that lift response rate from ~8% → ~18%:**
  - Specificity density: "I make a mean shakshuka on Sunday mornings" beats "I love cooking"
  - Self-aware humor: "I'm employed, emotionally available, and regularly call my mom"
  - Fantasy painting: "Imagine us dropping everything for that Greek island…"
  - End each answer with a hook a stranger can comment on
- **Avoid:** "partner in crime", "fluent in sarcasm", "good vibes only", listing adjectives without stories, self-deprecation that's actually insecurity.

## Voice + Video Prompt Edge

- Voice prompts: **32% more likely to lead to dates** (T2 Hinge)
- Video prompts: **62% more likes** than photo-only profiles (T2 platform data)
- Adoption is <5% — every voice/video prompt you record is a competitive moat
- 30s is enough; sound natural, don't perform

## "Never Married, No Kids" Framing on Hinge Specifically

- **Approach:** Weave into a prompt answer naturally rather than declaring it as bio fact.
- **Example:** "The one thing you should know about me is… I'm 41, never married, no kids — and genuinely excited about changing that."
- **Why it works on Hinge:** Hinge's audience reads profiles linearly. A pre-emptive, forward-intent framing eliminates the #1 question women 40+ ask. 80%+ ask about marital/kid status early — answering it inside a prompt makes the topic feel handled, not interrogated.
- **Don't:** Lead the entire profile with this fact, get defensive, or add "no baggage."

## 2026 Voice Trend: "Confident Vulnerability"

- **Source:** T2/T3 — Hinge data + cultural trend reporting
- **Stat:** 48% of Gen Z men hold back emotions to avoid seeming "too much"
- **Implication:** Profiles showing genuine personality + minor imperfections outperform polished ones. "Clear-coding" (saying what you mean without irony) is the named 2026 trend.
- **Translation for prompts:** Be specific about what you actually feel/want. Drop the smirky deflection.

## Effort Beats Income (Hinge-Adjacent)

- **Source:** T2 Hinge Labs survey
- **Stat:** 67% of women prefer effort over income; 84% prefer well-planned dates over expensive ones
- **Profile implication:** Show planning/curation/care in your prompts — "the best spot in town" type prompts are perfect — rather than status display.

## Confidence Notes / Caveats

- Hinge's 47% methodology is opaque (not independently replicated). Treat as directionally true, not a precise number.
- Most Hinge-specific data is platform-internal. Cross-reference with T1/T3 academic where possible.
- Childfree / never-married specific data on Hinge (vs. industry-wide) is thin — most findings generalize from broader studies.
