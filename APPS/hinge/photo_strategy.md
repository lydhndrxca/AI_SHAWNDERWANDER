# Hinge — Photo Strategy

> Why Hinge's photo logic is different from every other app, and how to lay out your 6 slots specifically for Hinge's UI and algorithm.

## Why Hinge photos play by different rules

Three Hinge-specific mechanics shift the optimal photo strategy:

1. **Comment-on-photo feature** — women like *individual* photos with a comment, not the whole profile. Photos that invite a comment beat photos that just look good.
2. **Linear / scrollable profile** — viewers see your photos *interleaved* with prompts as they scroll. Photo + prompt pairing is a real lever, unlike on Tinder.
3. **Slow-read audience** — Hinge users spend more time per profile than on swipe apps. Subtle details get noticed; weird-in-a-good-way photos pay off.

Net effect: **on Hinge, "interesting and comment-able" beats "objectively hottest."**

## The 6-slot framework for Hinge

| Slot | Purpose | Hinge-specific notes |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| **1** | Strong, clear, smiling face | Universal lead. Don't get cute here — Hinge first impressions still gate everything. |
| **2** | Conversation-magnet photo | The weirdest *good* photo you have. This is the comment-bait slot. |
| **3** | Activity / hobby with a question hook | Camping, instrument, sport, craft — something that prompts "tell me about this." |
| **4** | Dog / warmth / family | Hinge audience filters for relationship-readiness. Warmth signal here is non-negotiable. |
| **5** | Social proof — friends, especially women friends | Reduces "is he safe?" friction; opens easy comment territory ("love your group"). |
| **6** | Wildcard / travel / dressed up | Range signal. Last impression sets the closing tone. |

## What wins on Hinge

- **Comment-able weirdness** — A pop-tart pendant, an unusual setting, a moment mid-activity. The audience values "huh, what's the story" more than "wow, hot."
- **Specificity** — Identifiable place, identifiable activity, identifiable instrument. Generic = invisible.
- **Caption-able pairings** — A photo paired with a prompt that recontextualizes it (Hinge offers photo prompts; use one).
- **Lifestyle integration** — Photos where your interests are visible without being staged.
- **Genuine smiles** — Photofeeler T1: genuine smile beats neutral by a measurable margin on every metric.

## What loses on Hinge

- **Glamour shots without context** — Hot but boring = swipe-past on Hinge specifically (works better on Tinder).
- **Group photos as lead** — Slot 1 must be unambiguously you.
- **All-indoor or all-outdoor lineups** — Diversity of setting reads as "engaged life."
- **Sunglasses / hat / obscured face in slot 1** — −12% likes baseline; on Hinge specifically the comment feature can't grab a face it can't see.
- **Posing on camera** — Hinge audience is skilled at detecting "trying."
- **Mirror selfies in 2026** — Always read poorly; Hinge audience reads them worst.

## Pairing photos with prompts

Hinge has a *photo prompt* feature — a prompt overlaid on a photo. Use exactly one. Best uses:

| Photo type | Prompt to pair |
|------------|---------------|
| Weird object photo (pendant, costume, unusual setting) | "Two truths and a lie" or "Don't hate me if I…" |
| Activity photo (instrument, camping, sport) | "I geek out on…" or "Together, we could…" |
| Dog photo | "I'll fall for you if…" or just leave it (dogs sell themselves) |
| Travel photo | "I know the best spot in town for…" |
| Career photo (set, studio, etc.) | "I'm convinced that…" + a specific opinion from your work |

## Order matters more on Hinge than elsewhere

Tinder users see only photo #1. Hinge users see all 6 plus 3 prompts in a single scroll. The narrative arc you build matters:

- **Hook → Curiosity → Lifestyle → Warmth → Social → Range** is the canonical arc.
- Avoid stacking similar photos back-to-back (two studio shots in a row = redundant).
- Alternate indoor/outdoor, solo/social, candid/composed.

## Verification + completeness

Both are massive Hinge algorithmic levers (covered in `strategy.md`):

- **Verify** your account → +30–93% match rate (varies by source)
- **Fill all 6 photo slots** (minimum 4, but 6 is required for max algorithm weight)
- **No empty prompt slots**

## A/B testing on Hinge

If you have analytics access (Hinge Premium shows some data):

1. Lead photo: `your_strongest_face` vs. `your_strangest_good_photo` — compare comment rate, not just likes
2. Slot 6 swap: warm closing vs. range-signal closing — compare conversation-start rate
3. Pair the same activity photo with two different prompts in different weeks — compare comment specificity
