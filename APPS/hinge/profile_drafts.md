Three different profile *vibes*, each tuned for a different filter on Hinge. Each profile gives you **3 prompt slots × 10 response options** to pick from, plus **5 audio prompt ideas**. Pick one per slot to lock in.

**Anti-AI commitments applied throughout:**

- Sentence fragments allowed (and used)

- Drawn from your actual `MY_PROFILE.md` answers — pilot license, 30 plants, Walter, Idea Graveyard, ~200 kids in a gym, the cabin up north, the band, the Stradivarius/Little Ice Age fact, your real comedy taste, modern apartment opinion, In-N-Out hill, etc.

### Tier 3 — The 3-prompt structure (universal but tuned for Hinge)

Hinge gives you 3 written slots. Strongest combination for men 40+:

1. **Humor prompt** — observational or self-deprecating; shows personality without trying
2. **Depth prompt** — values, real interest, or one vivid scene
3. **Conversation-starter prompt** — direct invitation, debatable opinion, or open question

Each profile in this doc follows that structure (humor → depth → invitation, in roughly that order).

### Tier 4 — The "never married, no kids" framing (41M-specific)

This is your biggest non-photo lever and it's a *bio* problem, not a *prompt* problem. Women's default reaction to a never-married 41M is bifurcated — **"clean slate"** (positive) vs. **"commitment-phobe"** (red flag). Your profile controls which reading wins.

**Rules from research:**
1. **State it proactively** — eliminates the #1 question women 40+ ask men 40+ (80%+ ask about marital/kid status within first 3 messages)
2. **Pair with forward intent** — "Never married, no kids. Ready for the real thing." Or simply set Family Plans → "Want children" / Dating Intentions → "Long-Term Relationship" in vitals (Hinge displays these prominently — done correctly, you don't need to mention it in a prompt at all)
3. **Don't over-explain** — "I was focused on my career" reads as defensive. The vital + a strong warm profile = sufficient
4. **Let the profile prove relationship-readiness** — warmth, specificity, and the audio prompt do the work

**Practical recommendation for your profile:** since your Hinge vitals already include Family Plans = "Want Children" and Dating Intentions = "Long-Term Relationship," you don't need a "never married, no kids" line *in any prompt*. The vital handles it. Use the prompt slots for personality.

### Tier 5 — Anti-patterns that actively hurt (from `STRATEGY.md`)

These are statistically observed mistakes — every one of them lowers match rate measurably:

- ❌ **Lists of adjectives** ("fun, adventurous, loyal, ambitious") — reads as a résumé, signals nothing specific
- ❌ **"Just ask"** or empty prompts — costs you the slot AND the algorithm slot
- ❌ **Negativity** — "don't swipe if…", "tired of…", "no drama" — even framed as humor, reads as red flag
- ❌ **Clichés** — "fluent in sarcasm," "looking for my partner in crime," "shenanigans" — instant skip
- ❌ **Bragging about income/possessions** — even subtle ("just bought my third Tesla") — research shows this *negatively* correlates with women's interest at the conversion stage
- ❌ **Symmetrical triplet sentences** ("Coffee, conversation, and curiosity") — the AI tell. Triggers uncanny valley even when human-written
- ❌ **Long prompts that fill the 150-char limit** — research suggests sweet spot is 80–130 chars; max-length prompts read as effortful

### Tier 6 — The "light AI" finding (relevant to this doc)

Research shows:

The single biggest tell that something is AI is when the user copy-pastes without modifying. A 10% rewrite in your real cadence flips this from "AI bio" to "human bio with AI assist."
