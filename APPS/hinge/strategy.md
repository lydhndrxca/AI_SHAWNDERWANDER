# Hinge — Strategy Playbook

> Hinge-specific tactics extracted from `APPS/STRATEGY.md`, plus synthesized guidance.

## The Core Insight

**Prompts ARE your profile.** No separate bio exists. Every word in your three prompt answers does triple duty:
1. Signals personality
2. Generates a comment hook (the algorithm rewards commented likes)
3. Decides whether a like converts to a date (Layer 2 conversion)

Treat prompt slots like ad copy: scarcity is brutal, every line earns its place.

## The 3-Prompt Combo

For your three displayed prompts, use this combination:

1. **Humor prompt** — shows personality, makes them smile
2. **Depth prompt** — reveals values, goals, or genuine passion
3. **Conversation-starter** — makes it dead easy to message you

This isn't a rule you can break by accident — running 3 humor prompts reads as deflective; 3 depth prompts reads as heavy. Mix the registers.

## Underused-Prompt Strategy

Overused prompts ("My simple pleasure" answered with coffee, "Two truths and a lie" with travel facts) blur into noise. Pick prompts where most answers are bad — your good answer stands out more.

**Underused / high-leverage prompts:**
- "Don't hate me if I…"
- "The way to win me over is…"
- "I geek out on…"
- "Together, we could…"
- "Worst idea I've ever had…"
- "My most controversial opinion…"
- "I'm convinced that…"

## Use Voice / Video Prompts

- Adoption is **under 5%** → automatic differentiation
- Voice prompt: **+32% date conversion**
- Video prompt: **+62% likes**
- Don't perform. 30s, conversational, one specific story or take. Lo-fi authenticity beats studio polish.

## Comment-Driven Likes Are the Game

Hinge's algorithm weights **commented likes** highest. To engineer comments:

- End every prompt with a specific detail that's easy to react to
- Pair a photo prompt with a *strange* photo (the photo itself becomes the comment hook)
- Use specific numbers / nouns / proper names rather than abstractions ("I make shakshuka every Sunday" → people comment with "where's your recipe?")

## "Never Married, No Kids" Framing

| Where | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| Hinge | Weave into a prompt answer naturally: *"The one thing you should know about me is... I'm 41, never married, no kids — and genuinely excited about changing that."* |

**Rules:**
1. State it proactively (eliminates 80%+ of women's first-message questions)
2. Pair with forward intent ("want both" / "ready for the real thing")
3. Let the rest of the profile prove it (warmth, humor, specificity)
4. Don't over-explain ("I was focused on my career" reads defensive)

## Tone Match

| App | Vibe | Tone to Match |
|-----|------|---------------|
| **Hinge** | Relationship-focused | Thoughtful, genuine, specific |

Compared to Tinder (witty, brief) or Feeld (direct, playful), Hinge rewards a register that's *warm, considered, and specific*. Not earnest-cringe — earnest with edges.

## Timing

|-----|-----------|-------|

Timing is a marginal lever; profile quality swamps it. But peak-hour activity is free.

## Multi-App Stack

- **Primary (most effort):** Hinge + Bumble — best fit for relationship-oriented 41M
- Hinge is where to invest the most curation effort because the profile-comparison surface is largest (slow read, prompts visible, comment feature) — every detail is observable.

## What Doesn't Work on Hinge

- Lists of adjectives ("fun, adventurous, loyal, ambitious")
- "Just ask" or empty bios (you'd think this is obvious — half of male profiles do it)
- Negativity ("don't swipe if…", "tired of…")
- Clichés ("partner in crime," "fluent in sarcasm")
- Bragging about income/possessions
- Prompts answered with one word
- Sarcasm-as-personality (cool-distant register; doesn't sell on Hinge)

## What Works on Hinge

- **Specificity density** — "I make a mean shakshuka on Sunday mornings" >>> "I love cooking"
- **Conversation hooks** — questions, "hot takes," debate invitations
- **Active verbs** — "I'm training for a half marathon" vs. "I like running"
- **Narrative micro-snippets** — one specific story beats five generic claims
- **Photo-prompt pairs** — pair a weird/curious photo with a prompt that recontextualizes it
