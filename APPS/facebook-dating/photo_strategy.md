# Facebook Dating — Photo Strategy

> Why FB Dating's 12-slot photo+prompt grid plays differently from other apps.

## What's different on FB Dating

Three platform mechanics shift the optimal photo strategy:

1. **12 combined slots** — photos and prompts share a single 12-card grid. You can choose how many of each (recommended: 6 photos + 3 prompts = 9 used).
2. **Instagram pulls in alongside your photos** — viewers see your recent IG posts as a soft "context layer" beyond the curated 6.
3. **Shared groups / events / friends are visible** — they see "you both like [X group]" before they ever look at your photos. The photo's job is therefore *confirmation*, not *introduction*.

Net effect: **on FB Dating, the right photo is the photo your community would caption "yep, that's him."** Less polish. More naturalism. Local context wins.

## The 6-photo framework for FB Dating

| Slot | Purpose | FB Dating-specific notes |
|------|---------|--------------------------|
| **1** | Warm, approachable face photo | NOT the "hottest" photo — the friendliest one. FB audience filters for "would I want to know him" before "is he hot." |
| **2** | Full body / lifestyle | Outdoor, in-context, doing something casual. No Tinder gym-mirror energy. |
| **3** | Activity / hobby with regional flavor | Madison-specific or Wisconsin-flavored if possible (lakefront, cabin, local spot). Geographic context = community signal. |
| **4** | Community / friends / family | Facebook Dating audience trusts community signal more than swipe-app audiences. Visible social context here is high-leverage. |
| **5** | Creative / interests | Pick the one that makes you specific (band shot, miniatures set, instrument, woodworking, etc.). |
| **6** | Closing / range / travel | Last impression. Travel, dressed up, dog/family — whichever rounds out the picture. |

## What wins on FB Dating

- **Photos that look like real life** — slight imperfection > studio polish
- **Group/community photos** earlier in the lineup than on swipe apps (slot 3 or 4 is fine here, not slot 5+)
- **Geographic specificity** — Madison/Wisconsin landmarks, local restaurants, lakefront, regional context
- **One photo with kids in it (nieces/nephews)** if you have one — FB Dating audience reads family signal positively, especially for 40+ profiles
- **Instagram connection on** — adds a 7th–9th visual layer for free
- **Captions on photos** — FB Dating allows them; one specific caption ("first time at this place / with this person") beats no caption

## What loses on FB Dating

- All polished, all studio, all dressed-up
- All-solo photos (looks lonely; swipe apps tolerate this, FB doesn't)
- Mirror selfies in 2026 — universal lose
- Sunglasses or hats in slot 1 (−12% match rate baseline; FB community-style amplifies this loss)
- Same setting / same week stacking (audience pattern-matches "from one shoot" instantly)
- Photos with people who could be read as ex-partners (unique-to-FB risk: shared mutuals exist)

## Pairing photos with the prompt slots

You have 3 prompt slots in your 12-card grid. Best practice:

| Prompt slot | Pair with photo of | Why |
|-------------|--------------------|-----|
| Prompt 1 | Activity/lifestyle photo nearby | Keeps energy concrete |
| Prompt 2 | Friends/community photo nearby | Reinforces community signal |
| Prompt 3 | Creative/quirky photo nearby | Closes with personality |

## Order matters less here than on Hinge

FB Dating users browse profiles linearly but the shared-context surface (groups/Instagram) anchors first impressions before the grid. So while photo #1 still matters most, photos 2–6 have more equal weight than they would on Hinge or Tinder.

That means: **don't waste slot 6 on something filler.** Closing photos get more attention here than on other apps.
