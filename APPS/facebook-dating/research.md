# Facebook Dating — Research Findings

> Distilled from `APPS/RESEARCH.md`. Every Facebook Dating-specific data point with confidence and source tier.

## Headline Findings

### 1. 21.5M daily users — and growing
- **Confidence:** Medium-High
- **Source:** T2, Global Dating Insights (globaldatinginsights.com FB Dating)
- **Implication:** Massive untapped user base relative to competitive density. Not a fringe platform anymore.
- **Note:** Free model + Meta's user base = lower competition per profile than Hinge/Bumble/Tinder.

### 2. Predicted engagement scoring is the algorithm
- **Confidence:** High
- **Source:** T2 Meta transparency docs (transparency.meta.com/fb-dating)
- **How it works:** Algorithm predicts how likely you and a candidate are to engage (message, like, respond), weighted by **shared interests** and **shared groups/events**.
- **Implication:** Profile completeness, accurate interest tags, and joining real Facebook Groups (not just listing them) directly increase your match surface.

### 3. Community-driven traction in 2026
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Source:** T3 Reddit/Forum analysis (r/datingoverforty, r/OnlineDating)
- **Finding:** Community-reported sentiment shifted positive in 2025–2026 — "more natural" matching due to shared context. Particularly strong for users 30+.
- **Implication:** The audience increasingly skews toward people who actively dislike swipe-app dynamics. Friendly/relatable profiles outperform high-curation profiles here.

### 4. Bio length: longer wins (within reason)
- **Source:** T2 RESEARCH.md cross-platform analysis
- **Finding:** "Relationship-oriented apps (Hinge, CMB, FB Dating) reward substance" — short bios that work on Tinder under-perform here.
- **Sweet spot:** 3–5 short sentences. Density (specificity per word) matters more than raw length.

### 5. Free model = no paywall on visibility
- **Confidence:** High
- **Source:** T2 industry analysis (fastcompany.com Match Group revenue, etc.)
- **Implication:** Unlike Hinge/Tinder/Bumble where premium tiers gate match volume, FB Dating gives equal algorithmic treatment to every user. Profile quality is the ENTIRE lever.

## Two-Layer Model on FB Dating

The general rule (photos gate / text converts) still applies, but with a Facebook-specific twist:

- **Layer 1 (gate)** — Photos still drive initial attention.
- **Layer 1.5 (context)** — Shared groups/events/Instagram give viewers context BEFORE they read your bio. This layer is unique to FB Dating.
- **Layer 2 (conversion)** — Bio + prompts + Instagram posts decide whether they message.

The presence of layer 1.5 means: **a "thin" profile (no shared context, no group memberships) under-performs on FB Dating regardless of how good the bio is.** The fix is participation in real groups/events, not text rewrites.

## Audience Skew (community-reported, T3)

- **Age skew:** Slightly older than Hinge (more 35–55), with Hinge skewing 25–40
- **Selection bias:** Users have explicitly opted into Meta's ecosystem. More casual / community / "tired of swipe apps" energy
- **Family signal weight:** Higher than swipe apps. Users see your Facebook context (events, group affiliations), so family/community signals are amplified
- **Catfish risk:** Lowest of any platform — profiles are tied to verifiable Facebook accounts

## "Never Married, No Kids" Framing on FB Dating

- **Source:** T2 STRATEGY.md positioning section
- **Approach:** Natural-toned, integrated into bio rather than declared as a fact
- **Example phrasing:** *"41, no kids, never been married. Looking for someone who makes boring errands fun."*
- **Why it works on FB Dating:** The audience expects warmth and naturalness. Stating it inside the bio as one of multiple personal facts (not as an isolated declaration) reads as secure, not defensive.
- **Don't:** Make it the headline. Don't say "no baggage." Don't get cute about it.

## Bio Anti-Patterns (general + FB-specific)

From `RESEARCH.md` Finding 17 + STRATEGY.md:

**Avoid:**
- "Partner in crime"
- "Fluent in sarcasm"
- "Good vibes only"
- Listing adjectives ("fun, adventurous, loyal, ambitious")
- Self-deprecation that's actually insecurity
- "Just ask" / empty bio
- Negativity ("don't message me if…")
- Explicit DTF / hookup language (FB Dating audience reads this terribly)
- Performative humor — too-clever lines that feel like ad copy
- Bragging about income / car / lifestyle

**Win patterns:**
- **Specificity density** — "I make a mean shakshuka on Sunday mornings" beats "I love cooking"
- **Active verbs** — "I'm training for a half marathon" vs. "I like running"
- **Self-aware humor** rooted in truth, not performance
- **One specific opinion or hot take** as a conversation hook
- **Short sentences with idiosyncratic rhythm** — slightly broken cadence reads as human, not AI
- **Concrete nouns** — names, places, objects, instruments, foods

## What Generally Wins on FB Dating

| Lever | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Complete every profile field** | Algorithm weights completeness highly |
| **Connect Instagram** | Adds context layer; recent posts are conversation hooks |
| **Use the Secret Crush feature** | Up to 9 existing FB friends — uniquely high-conversion |
| **Join 2–3 real interest groups before going live** | Algorithm uses shared groups; matching pool widens dramatically |
| **List specific interests, not categories** | "Pickleball" beats "sports"; "miniature filmmaking" beats "creative" |
| **Lead with friendly, approachable photo** | Audience filters harder for warmth than swipe apps |
