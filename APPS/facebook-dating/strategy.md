# Facebook Dating — Strategy Playbook

> Tactical guidance synthesized from `APPS/STRATEGY.md` + research findings.

## The Core Insight

**Facebook Dating is the "context wins" app.** Unlike Hinge (prompts win), Tinder (photos win), or The League (credentials win), FB Dating's killer feature is **shared real-world context** — groups, events, friends-of-friends, Instagram posts. The optimal profile leans into that context rather than trying to dazzle in isolation.

Tone target: **friendly, natural, relatable.** Less "performance" than other apps. Warmth and approachability win.

## Tone Match (vs. other apps)

| App | Vibe | Tone to Match |
|-----|------|---------------|
| Tinder | Casual / fun / fast | Witty, confident, brief |
| Bumble | Intentional / balanced | Warm, approachable, conversation-ready |
| Hinge | Relationship-focused | Thoughtful, genuine, specific |
| **Facebook Dating** | **Casual / community** | **Friendly, natural, relatable** |
| The League | Ambitious / elite | Sharp, accomplished, concise |
| Feeld | Open-minded / exploratory | Direct, playful, authentic |
| CMB | Serious / quality-focused | Detailed, sincere, polished |

The biggest tonal divergence: **don't try to be witty here.** Hinge / Tinder reward sharp punchlines. FB Dating rewards "I would actually be friends with this person." Lean warm; let one specific concrete detail carry the personality work.

## Bio Architecture (3–5 short sentences, ≤500 chars)

**The bio is for voice, not metadata.** Age, location, height, marital status, and "wants kids" are all already filled in by FB Dating's structured profile fields. Repeating them in the bio is wasted space and makes you read like a LinkedIn profile.

What the bio actually needs to do:

1. **Sound like a real person mid-life**, not a profile being presented
2. **Show one specific thing about how you live** through concrete nouns and details, not adjectives
3. **Have a voice** — distinctive rhythm, slight idiosyncrasy, an actual point of view
4. **Be easy to react to** without an explicit CTA

Five formats that work better than the default stat sheet:

| Format | Move |
|--------|------|
| **Cold Open** | Start mid-sentence ("...so what I was getting at is the wood…") |
| **Vignette** | Paint one specific scene in present tense |
| **Inventory** | List physical objects from your actual life — not adjectives |
| **Manifesto** | One strong opinion, briefly defended; filter hard |
| **Personal Ad parody** | "WANTED: curious accomplice for…" — formal genre + playful body |

See `bios.md` for fully drafted examples of each.

Avoid:
- Direct CTAs ("message me if…", "let's grab coffee") — transactional on this platform
- Listing 5+ hobbies — concrete nouns embedded in a scene > a hobby list
- Generic value claims ("I value honesty") — show, don't tell
- Stat-sheet openers ("I'm 41, live in [city]…") — your structured profile already says this
- Anything that reads as ad copy

## Algorithm Levers (free wins, ranked)

1. **Fill every profile field** — algorithm weights completeness highly
2. **Connect Instagram** — adds visual context beyond your 6 photos and gives matches recent-life material
3. **Join 2–3 active Facebook Groups** related to your real interests (Madison-area, gaming, music, woodworking, golf, etc.) before going live — directly expands matching pool via shared-group signal
4. **Use the Secret Crush feature** — up to 9 existing Facebook friends. This is unique to FB Dating and the highest-conversion match path the platform offers
5. **Set accurate interests/preferences** — these feed the predicted-engagement model
6. **Don't ghost** — algorithm penalizes low response rates over time

## Multi-App Position

From STRATEGY.md:
- **Primary (most effort):** Hinge + Bumble
- **Secondary:** CMB or **FB Dating** — lower effort, different audience
- **Effort target:** ~30 min once to set up; 10 min/week to maintain

FB Dating is a **secondary app** in the stack — you should not be putting Hinge-level curation hours into it. The audience rewards naturalness; over-polished bios actively under-perform.

## Photo Strategy (high-level)

See `photo_strategy.md` for detail. The short version:

- 6 photos lead the 12-slot photo+prompt grid
- Lead with the **warmest** face photo, not the hottest one (FB Dating audience filters for "would I be friends with this guy")
- Include at least one community/social photo (friends, family, group setting) — this app rewards visible community ties
- Include local/Madison-specific imagery if you have it (favorite spots, regional context) — boosts geographic matching
- Keep one wildcard / hobby shot to feed conversation hooks

## "Never Married, No Kids" Framing on FB Dating

| App | Approach |
|-----|----------|
| **FB Dating** | **Natural tone integrated into bio: "41, no kids, never been married. Looking for someone who makes boring errands fun."** |

**Why this framing wins here:**
- Says it without making it the headline
- "Open to both" or implicit-via-soft-hook neutralizes the commitment-phobe read
- Casual register (no kids, never been married) matches the community vibe — feels like a friend telling you about himself, not a profile selling you on himself

## Anti-Patterns (don't do these on FB Dating)

- Empty bio + 6 polished photos (reads "I'm just here for looks")
- Stack of vague claims ("foodie, traveler, dog lover") — FB Dating audience instinctively distrusts these
- Sarcasm-as-personality
- Long earnest paragraphs about your dating philosophy
- Calling yourself "a hopeless romantic" or any version of self-soft-marketing
- Photos all from the same week / event (algorithm reads it; audience reads it too)

## What Wins on FB Dating

- A bio that sounds like you wrote it on the bus
- One vivid, specific image of how you actually live
- Visible community ties (groups, events, friends in photos)
- Instagram connection with recent posts
- Geographic specificity — "Madison-based, near downtown" beats "in the Midwest"
- A single closing line that's easy for a stranger to comment on
