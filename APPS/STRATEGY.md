Data-backed research on what actually works. Updated April 2026.

## Universal Rules (All Platforms)

### The 3-Second Rule
Users spend **3–7 seconds** on a profile before deciding. Your first photo and first visible text carry ~80% of the weight.

### Completeness Wins
- Complete profiles get **50% more matches** across all platforms
- Every empty field is a missed opportunity and a signal of low effort

### Algorithm Awareness (2026)
Modern dating apps use **Engagement Depth Analysis**:
- They track how long people stay on your profile
- Whether they expand/read your bio
- Your response speed to matches
- Active users who engage with matches get boosted

## Photo Strategy

### The Ideal Photo Lineup (5–6 photos)

| Slot | Type | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| 1 | **Clear headshot** — facing camera, natural smile, no sunglasses/hats | 102% more likes vs. obscured face |
| 2 | **Full-body shot** — natural setting, shows how you actually look | Builds trust, avoids "catfish" anxiety |
| 3 | **Activity/hobby shot** — doing something you love | Shows personality beyond appearance |
| 4 | **Social photo** — with friends (you clearly identifiable) | Social proof, shows you have a life |
| 5 | **Pet / travel / candid laugh** — warm, approachable energy | Emotional connection trigger |
| 6 | **Wildcard** — dressed up, adventure, cooking, etc. | Shows range |

### Photo Do's
- Natural outdoor lighting (golden hour or overcast = best)
- High resolution
- Wear colors that contrast with background
- Genuine smile showing teeth
- Eye contact with camera in at least 2 photos
- Mix of environments (not all in same room)

### Photo Don'ts
- Bathroom mirror selfies (**40–90% fewer likes**)
- Sunglasses in primary photo (triggers distrust)
- Group photo as first image
- Gym mirror flex shots
- Photos older than 12 months
- Heavy filters / face-tuning
- Every photo a selfie
- Blurry / dark / pixelated images
- Photos with ex cropped out (always obvious)

### Photo Count Sweet Spot
- **5–6 photos** is optimal across all platforms
- Fewer than 3 = low effort signal
- Using all 9 (on Tinder/CMB) can dilute quality and signal desperation

## Bio & Prompt Strategy

### Bio Formula (Universal)
1. **Hook** — something unexpected, funny, or intriguing in the first line
2. **Substance** — what you actually do / care about (specific > generic)
3. **Invitation** — end with something that makes replying easy

### What Works
- **Specificity** — "I make a mean shakshuka on Sunday mornings" beats "I love cooking"
- **Humor** — self-deprecating > bragging, observational > try-hard
- **Vulnerability** — one honest/real thing mixed with lightness
- **Conversation hooks** — questions, "hot takes," debate invitations
- **Active verbs** — "I'm training for a half marathon" vs. "I like running"

### What Doesn't Work
- Lists of adjectives ("fun, adventurous, loyal, ambitious")
- "Just ask" or empty bios
- Negativity ("don't swipe if...", "tired of...")
- Clichés ("looking for my partner in crime", "fluent in sarcasm")
- Bragging about income/possessions
- Too long — no one reads a novel

### The 3-Prompt Strategy (Hinge / Bumble / Tinder)
For apps with 3 prompt slots, use this combo:
1. **Humor prompt** — shows personality, makes them smile
2. **Depth prompt** — reveals values, goals, or genuine passion
3. **Conversation-starter** — makes it dead easy to message you

## Platform-Specific Tactics

### Tinder
- Bio sweet spot: **15–45 words** — ultra-concise
- Prompts are relatively new — many profiles still don't use them, so filling yours = instant differentiation
- First photo is EVERYTHING (swipe decision happens before bio)
- Humor and confidence play well here

### Bumble
- Women message first — your profile needs to give them something to open with
- Bio (300 chars) should include a clear conversation hook / question
- Opening Moves feature: if you match, the pre-set question appears — think of this as a soft opener
- Slightly more relationship-oriented audience than Tinder

### Hinge
- **Prompts ARE your profile** — no separate bio, so prompt answers must carry all the weight
- Likes on prompts **47% more likely to lead to dates** than likes on photos
- Use underused prompts — overused ones get lost in a sea of similar answers
- Video and voice prompts stand out dramatically (most people don't use them)
- Each prompt answer is an opportunity for someone to comment on specifically

### Feeld
- Audience is open-minded, sex-positive, and direct
- Be upfront about what you're looking for — ambiguity doesn't play well here
- "Desires" tags are crucial — they're how people filter
- Tone can be more playful/provocative than other apps
- Couples and individuals both active — state what you're open to

### Coffee Meets Bagel
- Limited daily matches = your profile has to convert on first impression
- Photo captions are unique to CMB — use them as mini-prompts
- Icebreakers matter — a good one increases reply rate significantly
- More relationship-focused audience
- Detail matters: fill in every field (occupation, school, etc.)

### The League
- **190 chars is brutal** — every word must earn its place
- LinkedIn/career signals matter — this app's audience cares about ambition
- 4-part formula works: Hook → Credibility → Personality → CTA
- Photos should look polished/professional but not stiff
- Group events and video dates are differentiators — use them

### Facebook Dating
- Underused platform = less competition
- Integration with Facebook groups/events is the killer feature — shared context makes matching feel natural
- Secret Crush feature is uniquely powerful (match with people you already know)
- Instagram integration — pull your best posts
- Bio can be longer (500 chars) — use the space for warmth and specificity

## "Never Married, No Kids" Positioning (41M-Specific)

### The Core Insight

Women's default reaction to a never-married 41-year-old is bifurcated: **"clean slate"** (positive) vs. **"commitment-phobe"** (red flag). Your profile controls which reading wins.

### Framing Rules

1. **State it proactively** — eliminates the #1 question women ask 40+ men (80%+ ask about marital/kid status early)
2. **Pair with forward intent** — "Never married, no kids. Ready for the real thing." or "Want both." signals eligibility, not avoidance
3. **Let the profile prove it** — warmth, humor, specificity, and emotional maturity in prompts/bio provide the behavioral evidence that you're relationship-ready
4. **Don't over-explain** — "I was focused on my career" reads as defensive. The phrase + strong profile is enough.

### What NOT to do

- Leave it unstated (creates suspicion, leads to interrogation)
- Use it as the entire bio identity (one data point, not your whole personality)
- Add "no baggage" (can sound like you're preemptively dismissing divorced people)
- Get defensive about it in conversations

### Platform-Specific Framing

| App | Approach |
|-----|----------|
| Tinder | Brief mention in bio: "41. Never married, no kids, want both. [humor hook]" |
| Bumble | Bio line + conversation hook: "No kids, never married — I was too busy [funny reason]. Now looking for someone worth slowing down for." |
| Hinge | Weave into a prompt answer naturally: "The one thing you should know about me is... I'm 41, never married, no kids — and genuinely excited about changing that." |
| Feeld | Less relevant — this audience cares about desires/openness, not marital history |
| CMB | State clearly in "About me" — this detail-oriented audience appreciates directness |
| The League | Subtle flex: "Never married, no kids. Career-first, now ready for what's next." |
| FB Dating | Natural tone: "41, no kids, never been married. Looking for someone who makes boring errands fun." |

## AI-Assisted Profile Writing (Optimal Approach)

### The "Light AI" Method

Fully AI-generated bios get -27% fewer matches. But human draft + AI polish gets +30% more than human-only.

### Process

1. **Freewrite (5 min):** Raw, unedited draft of what you'd say to a friend about yourself. Don't worry about length, grammar, or being clever.
2. **AI Refinement:** Use AI to trim, restructure, optimize word choice, and add hooks — while preserving YOUR specific details, humor, and voice.
3. **Human Review:** Read the AI output aloud. If it doesn't sound like you, revise. Keep only what feels authentic.
4. **Test + Iterate:** Run for 1-2 weeks, then swap one element at a time.

### Why This Works

- Human bios contain 4.2 concrete details per 100 words (AI: 1.8)
- Human bios include "constraint signaling" — admitting imperfections — 73% of the time (AI: 9%)
- Human self-disclosure to invitation ratio is 2.3:1 (AI: 5.1:1 — too much talking, not enough inviting)
- Your raw material provides what only you have; AI provides structure and polish

## Optimization Loop

Once profiles are live:

1. **Track matches per week** per platform
2. **Rotate first photo** every 5–7 days (test which gets most traction)
3. **Swap one prompt** at a time to isolate what works
4. **Note which prompts get comments** vs. just likes
5. **Check "recently active" or boost times** — post updates during peak hours (Sun–Thu, 6–10 PM)
6. **Monthly photo refresh** — new/updated photos give +28% algorithm visibility boost
7. **Review "light AI" approach** — re-run bios through AI quarterly with fresh context
