# Tinder — Strategy Playbook

> Tactical guidance synthesized from research + multi-platform context.

## The Core Insight

Tinder is the **headline-writing app**. Your photos get the swipe; your bio is read in 3–7 seconds. Treat it like an ad headline, not a personal essay. The constraint forces clarity.

For a 41M serious dater, Tinder is a **volume play, not a primary channel**. The audience skews younger/casual — your job is to filter HARD for the right slice of the userbase, fast.

## Tone Match

| App | Vibe | Tone to Match |
|-----|------|---------------|
| **Tinder** | **Casual / fun / fast** | **Witty, confident, brief** |
| Bumble | Intentional / balanced | Warm, approachable, conversation-ready |
| Hinge | Relationship-focused | Thoughtful, genuine, specific |
| FB Dating | Casual / community | Friendly, natural, relatable |

Tinder rewards the closest thing to **stand-up energy** of any app. Brevity, punchlines, opinions, edge — all encouraged. Earnestness — not so much.

## The 3-Trait Bio Framework (research-backed)

The high performers use this structure (or a variant). All three in 15–45 words:

1. **Competence signal** — one concrete maturity/stability beat
2. **Playful hook** — humor, weirdness, or spark
3. **Micro-flirt / invitation** — opens conversation without explicit CTA

Example: *"Engineer who can actually hold a conversation. I make incredible pancakes and questionable decisions. If you like dry humor and deep conversations, we'll get along."*

Apply that pattern to your material — don't copy the example.

## Bio Architecture

- **Word count:** 15–45 (Tinder's published optimal). Stay under 200 chars.
- **Sentence count:** 1–3 short sentences.
- **Open with the most distinctive thing about you** — your career arrow, your one weird hobby, your strongest opinion.
- **Close with a hook** — a fact someone wants to ask about, not a CTA telling them to message you.

## Prompt Strategy

You get **3 prompts × 124 chars each.** Pick three that hit different surfaces:

| Surface | Best prompts for it |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Voice / humor** | "Two truths and a lie", "I'm known for", "Don't hate me if I", "Typical Sunday" |
| **Filter** | "We'll get along if", "Change my mind about", "Green flags I look for", "I'm looking for" |
| **Hook / opinion** | "My most controversial opinion", "I won't shut up about", "I geek out on" |

Don't pick three of the same surface. Variety = signal of range.

## Algorithmic Levers

- **Verify the account** — +30% match rate. Free.
- **Don't swipe indiscriminately.** Selectivity is rewarded post-Elo. ~30–50% right-swipe rate is optimal.
- **First 24–72 hours after launch matter most.** Tinder gives a new-account visibility boost; your initial likes and matches calibrate your long-term ranking. Don't go live until everything's optimized.
- **Daily activity matters.** Brief, regular use beats marathon swiping sessions.
- **Reply velocity matters.** Slow responses tank your visibility over time.

## "Never Married, No Kids" Framing on Tinder

**Don't put it in the bio.** Tinder bios are too tight to spend a full sentence on demographics. Let:

- Your photos imply the maturity (lifestyle photos, not gym mirror)
- Your voice imply seriousness (well-written, not lazy)
- The structured profile fields (relationship goal, family plan) handle the disclosure

If you absolutely must address it: do it inside a prompt — "I'm looking for…" is the natural slot. *"Looking for the actual one. Yes, I know, never been married. The good ones rarely come with a Tinder track record."* — that kind of voice. But this is OPTIONAL on Tinder, unlike Hinge.

## What to Do, What Not to Do

| Do | Don't |
|------|-------|
| Use specific concrete nouns | Use abstract adjective lists |
| Lead with your most distinctive thing | Lead with "I'm 41 from [city]…" |
| One opinion or hot take | "No drama" / negativity |
| Self-aware humor | Self-deprecation that's actually insecurity |
| Acknowledge the absurdity of dating apps | Take the medium too seriously |
| Show range in 3 prompts | Run 3 humor prompts and no depth |
| Keep all three prompts under 100 chars (124 cap; tight is better) | Run up against the limit on every prompt |
