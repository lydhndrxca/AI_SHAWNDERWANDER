# Tinder — Photo Strategy

> Why Tinder photos play by the strictest rules of any app.

## What's different on Tinder

- **First photo decides 80%+ of swipes.** Decision is made before the bio is read.
- **3–7 second scan time.** Photos must communicate instantly; complexity loses.
- **Algorithm uses VLM photo ranking** (post-2024 update) — Face Check verification mandatory; image quality measurable.
- **Audience is broad/casual** — your job is to filter for the slice of serious daters via *signal density*, not curate a deeply specific aesthetic.

## The 6-photo Tinder framework

| Slot | Purpose | Tinder-specific notes |
|------|---------|------------------------|
| **1** | The hottest face photo | Slot 1 is 80% of the decision. Use your strongest photo, period — not your warmest, not your most interesting. **Hottest, with face clearly visible.** |
| **2** | Second-hottest, different setting | Stack attractiveness early. Indoor → outdoor change adds variety. |
| **3** | Variety / no-hat outdoor | Show the face fresh after two strong indoor shots. |
| **4** | Dog / family / warmth signal | Critical on Tinder for instant warmth differentiation — most male profiles don't have this. |
| **5** | Activity / career signal | Director's chair / film set / studio — one signal that screams "has a real life." |
| **6** | Fun / closing energy | Last impression. Disney joy, friend group laugh, or anything joyful. |

## What wins on Tinder

- **Hot lead photo, period.** No experiments in slot 1.
- **Smiling face, no sunglasses, no hats** in slot 1 (–12% likes baseline penalty)
- **Outdoor shots** outperform indoor — natural light + lifestyle context
- **Genuine smile beats neutral** by a measurable margin (Photofeeler T1 data)
- **Dog photos add ~8–10% match rate** on Tinder specifically
- **One clearly visible career/lifestyle photo** signals "has a real life" — shockingly underused by 40+ men

## What loses on Tinder

- **Mirror selfies** — universal lose; on Tinder specifically the audience reads them as "no one else takes my photo"
- **Group photos as lead** — slot 1 must be unambiguously you
- **Sunglasses / hats in slot 1** — algorithmic + behavioral penalty
- **Same-shoot stacking** — audience reads "from one event" instantly
- **Anything weird without context** — Hinge rewards quirky; Tinder doesn't have time for it
- **Costume photos** — unless you're at a wedding or similar
- **Gym mirror with shirt off** — wide-net negative for 35+ audience

## Algorithmic levers

- **Verify** the account — Face Check is mandatory in 2026; do it day 1
- **Fill all 9 slots if possible** — completeness rewarded; 6 minimum
- **Don't iterate photos in the first 30 days** — algorithm needs stable signal to calibrate
- **Use Tinder Boost during peak hours** (Sunday 6–9 PM) for max ROI

## A/B testing on Tinder

Slot 1 is too important to A/B-test casually. Don't experiment in slot 1; the lead photo is locked once you find the strongest. Test elsewhere:

1. Slot 6: `chat_04` (Disney) vs `IMG_2865` (park). Compare swipe-right rate.
2. Slot 5: career signal photo vs alternative activity. Compare match → message rate.

Don't change two photos at once — single-variable testing only.
