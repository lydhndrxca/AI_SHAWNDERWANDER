# Tinder — Research Findings

> Distilled from `APPS/RESEARCH.md` + April 2026 web research on Tinder bio best-practice.

## Headline Findings

### 1. Photos do 80%+ of the work; bio does the remainder
- **Confidence:** High
- **Source:** University of Amsterdam (T3) + SwipeStats analysis of 7,000+ profiles (T2)
- **Implication:** Bio cannot save bad photos, but a great bio reliably converts attention into right-swipes. **4x match rate** for profiles with bios vs. without.

### 2. The 3–7 second decision window
- **Confidence:** High
- **Source:** Multiple T2/T3 (SwipeStats, scimatch.com, etc.)
- **Implication:** Your bio must hit like a headline, not read like a paragraph. **15–45 words is the sweet spot** — Tinder's own published guidance.

### 3. Selectivity is rewarded by the algorithm
- **Confidence:** Medium-High
- **Source:** T3 algorithm analyses of post-Elo Dynamic Desirability
- **Implication:** Don't swipe right indiscriminately. The algorithm weights selective swiping; over-generous swipers get suppressed.

### 4. Humor is the great equalizer
- **Confidence:** Medium-High
- **Source:** SwipeStats, scimatch.com (T2/T3); academic work on humor as creative-intelligence signal
- **Implication:** **Humor signals creative intelligence and increases perceived attractiveness by up to 36%.** A bio that gets a laugh outperforms a bio that lists hobbies, every time.

### 5. The 3-Trait Bio Framework (high performers consistently use this)
- **Source:** Pattern analysis across 184+ high-performing male bios (T3)
- **Three components:**
  - **Competence Signal** — stability/maturity (e.g., "Engineer who can actually hold a conversation")
  - **Playful Hook** — humor or spark
  - **Micro-Flirt / Invitation** — opens conversation without explicit CTA

## What 41M Serious Daters Need to Know About Tinder

- **You're an outlier.** Most Tinder users skew younger / more casual. Bio needs to filter hard for serious daters or you'll waste time.
- **The maturity signal must NOT read as "settled and boring."** Best move is showing range — confident-and-fun without being immature, serious-about-life without being heavy.
- **Avoid "looking for serious relationship" in bio.** Reads as desperate. Show seriousness via what you DO and HOW you write, not by declaring intent.
- **The "never married, no kids" disclosure on Tinder:** Best to *not* state it explicitly here. Tinder bios are too short. Let it be inferred from voice + maturity + photos. The structured profile fields handle the demographics.

## What Wins on Tinder

- **Confident self-aware humor** — "I'm 6'2" because apparently that matters here"
- **Specificity** — "I make a carbonara that will make you forget your ex" beats "I love food"
- **Concrete nouns** — Pasta, dog, car, instrument, place. Not adjectives.
- **One opinion or hot take** — Filters effectively in 3-7 seconds
- **Self-aware acknowledgment of the medium** — "Can't believe I'm doing this but here we are" type lines
- **Range signal** — Show you have multiple sides without listing them as a tag cloud

## What Loses on Tinder

- **Empty bio** — 23% of users do this; you cannot afford to
- **Generic adjective lists** — "fun, adventurous, loyal, ambitious"
- **Clichés** — "fluent in sarcasm," "partner in crime," "good vibes only"
- **Negativity** — "no drama," "don't message me if you…"
- **Trauma dumping** — your divorce, your last relationship, your therapy backstory
- **Bragging about income / car / lifestyle**
- **The dreaded "ask me anything" / "just ask"** — the laziest possible bio
- **Earnest paragraphs** — Tinder bios are not the place for sincerity essays
